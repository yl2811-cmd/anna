The World of Archeon

Archeon is a sub-Earth-sized planet in a K2V star system, roughly 520 light-years from Earth. In the 25th century, an autonomous terraforming package — the CI (Colonial Infrastructure) — arrived and spent 30 years reshaping the continent of Aethelgard into a habitable zone: detonating polar ice caps, seeding engineered biomes, and building the skeletal infrastructure for a million-person colony.

Then, in 2506, Betelgeuse went supernova just 7 light-years away. The electromagnetic storm severed all interstellar communication. No more ships. No more signals. Earth became a memory.

521 years later, the colony has become something the original planners never imagined. The CI's nano-concrete spires still stand — but now they're wrapped in flowering vines and repurposed as housing blocks. The fusion reactors are dead, replaced by steam turbines and wind towers. The population of roughly one million lives on a single continent, connected not by networks or highways, but by a fleet of brass-and-canvas airships that thread between 21 settlements like blood vessels.

Their social fabric is called The Loom — not a government, not a market, but a craft-based meritocracy woven from apprenticeship, mutual obligation, and the shared understanding that on a world this small, every skill matters and nothing is disposable.

[ character portrait ]
Anna Freedman — Protagonist

Airship mechanic, pilot, and the story's protagonist. Her red-white-grey flight suit was sewn by her father Edmund using colors from Archeon's oldest craft traditions. The goggles and tool belt are standard-issue for canyon-rated mechanics. She is 19, quiet, good with her hands, and carries more weight than she knows.

[ cityscape · spires ]
Frontier City — Capital

Archeon's largest settlement (pop. ~250,000) and the hub of its airship network. CI-era nano-concrete towers rise above street-level markets where blacksmiths, printmakers, and salvage traders work in open-air workshops. Five centuries of human adaptation have turned sterile colonial infrastructure into something alive.

[ market district · dusk ]
The Brass Quarter

Steam from the bathhouses mixes with coal-smoke from Ironmonger Street. Every surface tells two stories — the precise geometry of CI-era engineering underneath, and the hand-welded, vine-covered improvisation of 500 years of human habitation on top.

[ elevated walkways ]
Upper Frontier — Walkways

Elevated walkways connect the upper levels of Frontier's repurposed CI towers. Below, the market district hums with the sound of hammer-on-anvil, the hiss of steam vents, and pilots bartering for docking priority. Archeon has no internet, no broadcast media — news travels at the speed of airships and word of mouth.

[ festival · banners ]
Thread Festival

A settlement square during the Thread Festival — Archeon's annual celebration of the Loom network. Colored banners stream between buildings, workshop teams compete in the Skeleton Derby (build a working device from scrap, fewest parts wins), and children run between food stalls selling black-iron chicory and honey pastries.

[ vertical farms ]
Vertical Farms — Frontier City

CI-era building shells have been converted into hydroponic gardens, their broken windows replaced with canvas awnings that filter Archeon's warm orange-white sunlight. Every settlement grows its own food — there is no industrial agriculture, only distributed cultivation woven into the architecture.

[ open plains · windmills ]
Iris Hollow — Agricultural Heartland

Archeon's agricultural heartland (pop. ~120,000). Unlike Frontier's vertical density, Iris sprawls across open plains dotted with wind turbines and solar collectors. During the harvest festival, skiff pilots drag colored streamers across the sky in a tradition called Sky Weaving — not a competition, but a collaborative aerial dance.

[ cliff edge · airships ]
Orun Plateau Edge — Outpost

A plateau-edge outpost overlooking the Orun Cliff — the 3-kilometer vertical wall separating the habitable Central Basin from the Great Ochre Desert below. Smaller settlements like Riverbend and Windbreak perch along this edge, serving as waypoints for airships crossing between the Basin and the southern frontier.

[ massive building · flowers ]
Wind Hall of Skyleaf

A settlement hidden inside a crater in the Serpent's Tooth mountains (pop. ~5,000). Originally a CI-era environmental monitoring station, it now houses Archeon's gene vault and a community of botanical researchers. The surrounding flower fields were planted by Edmund Freedman, Anna's father, 20 years ago — each color chosen to match the pigments used in Archeon's oldest craft traditions.

[ airship · canyon ]
Atheria Canyon System

A cargo skiff crossing the Atheria Canyon System — a network of slot canyons where Archeon's most skilled mechanics maintain the infrastructure that keeps settlements connected. The canyon walls amplify every sound: a trained mechanic can diagnose a failing turbine from the echo alone. This is where Anna grew up, and where she learned to listen.

Anna

Anna Freedman is 19 years old, an airship mechanic and pilot raised in Atheria Canyon. She is not a chosen one. She is not special in any dramatic sense. She is a young woman who is very good at fixing things, who listens more than she speaks, and who carries an unresolved knot of memory six years ago that she has never fully confronted.

Visual DNA

The aesthetic of Skies Beyond the Star lives at the intersection of three traditions: the pastoral warmth of Studio Ghibli, the mechanical detail of steampunk illustration, and the emotional precision of Violet Evergarden's visual storytelling. Archeon is not a dystopia. It is a civilization that chose to thrive within its constraints — and that choice shows in every hand-painted airship hull, every vine climbing a 500-year-old concrete wall, and every festival lantern lit against an orange-white sky.
